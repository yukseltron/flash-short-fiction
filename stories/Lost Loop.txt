"ERROR, IT CANNOT BE READ. EXPIRED SINCE: NINE-A-TWELVE. CENTURY A-SIX"

With a whirr, the machine spits back out a small grey card. The card is chipped and bent at the corners. Parts of it still retain its gloss, while most of it is skinned, exposing thin strands of plastic bleached by the sun pealing off. 

With shaking fingers, he gingerly grabs the card, quickly grooms it to satisfy the machine's taste, and tries again. 

"ERROR, IT CANNOT BE READ. EXPIRED SINCE: NINE-A-TWELVE. CENTURY A-SIX"

The repeated reply frustrates the man, and he wrenches a fist loose from his pockets. With a tightened grip, a flimsy punch is directed to the shiny metal screen of the machine. The machine remains unaffected and apathetic. In response it simply ejects the card back out. The man grabs it again and stuffs it away. He has other ideas.

"HELLO, PLEASE ENTER YOUR CARD."

Rummaging through his bag, a faint howl is heard in a distance. Barely audible, it grips the man's fear and attention. He listens diligently to confirm his fears, but the howl fades out. Anxious, he becomes frantic as he tosses all non essentials from his bag, looking for whatever he needs.

The machine looks on. Its screen, dulled from constant bleaching from the sun, emits but a soft glow as the sky darkens for the looming night. 

As he searches through his bag, he picks things he deems will be useful in the near future. A gun, a small ring, and a device unknown to most.

The soft howls return from the distance. No need for doubt this time, his instincts tell him to be wary.

They're coming.

A quick check to reassure himself there's ammunition, and the gun is put to his side, ready for use. He places the ring between his front teeth, and bites down hard. Hands shaking, he grips the mysterious device and activates it. Or maybe it activated itself. There was no clear action for how he did it. For an observer, it's as if the device just woke on its own.

Thin tendrils emerge from the device and wrap around his wrist. They're a cool black and glimmer from the machine's light. A snakes belly slithering to safety. Together they seamlessly tighten. The man can feel his blood halt as his hand goes numb. The device flips open with a static whir. A buzz that stabs his hears. It barely covers the sounds of hounds having caught the scent of their prey.

As the buzzing intensifies, it starts spinning rapidly. The force increases drastically, and the man realizes he is no longer gripping the device. The tendrils themselves make sure he never lets go. His teeth clamp down further into the ring as it too starts vibrating in response to the device. It does its best to wrestle free, but the man is more desperate to hold it in place. He closes his eyes as he lets the device do its magic. He's done this before, but it never gets easier. He never thought he'd have to use it so soon again.

Roars and howls come crashing through the horizon. A stampede of bloodthirst. All unified in a single competitive goal to take the man down. Hounds and killers and machines. They loom on their target, eager to grab hold of him. The sight of the device, their true prize, pushes them to rush faster. The man keeps his eyes shut, unwilling to turn and see them breathe down his neck. Praying for the machine to take him away. 

They come for him. Bearing teeth and knives. Incapable of focusing on anything else. Gaze fixed on the brink of insanity. Dying for the release.

A loud burst. Blinding light and heat erupting everywhere. A fuzz that tickles and trickles through your brain, down your stem, all throughout your body. 

He opens his eyes. He's gone. They're gone. It's all gone. Why is he back here again? Why did he use the device? His hand is empty. The device is gone. The black tendrils didn't even leave a mark. What was he holding before?

The machine beeps at him.

"HELLO, PLEASE ENTER YOUR CARD."

Without thinking, he takes out his card and follows as instructed.

"THANK YOU, YOU'VE BEEN ACCEPTED. PLEASE WAIT TO ENTER."

The screen darkens and the machine changes life.
