"I have something to tell you."

With a wave of his fingers, brilliant lights flash behind him in colourful patterns. An assortment of vivid pictures sublime for the mind. The audience gaze intently, trapped by their curiosity.

A smile, a snap of fingers, and everything is still. The pictures and lights fade to black. Faint murmurs and shuffles. The cool buzz of the AC. Sprinkles of dust flake down. A lone beam of light strike him at the podium as he stares intently at his gawking crowd. He has them right where he wants them.

"Do you ever feel... unsatisfied? As though the life you wish to have is within reach yet you cannot move your own arm to grab? As though some force field, a barrier, holds you back. Is that not frustrating?"


"Friends, do not doubt the potential hidden inside all of you. This mental barrier you cannot overcome exists for a simple reason. It is difficult to accept, but understand the wall in front of you is partially made by you. You want to be safe, to not fall and risk injury or worse."

The murmurs dissipate. The focus of the audience can almost be heard if not for the man breaking the silence to speak to his crowd.

"But this is ok. We all must overcome ourselves to be in control. It is a tough task indeed, but rest assured we are all capable. The potential resides in all of us and will not discriminate. In fact, the secret to unlocking it all, what could it be?"

He chuckles. He runs his fingers together as if pondering how he should phrase his next words. 

"Luckily, I've overcome this obstacle, like many before me. I can tell you a secret to overcome it, just like someone told me, like many before me."

Ears turn forward with attention, ready to grab the details of anything that comes out of his mouth next. The rush of a secret, of knowledge only grasped and kept by few is about to be heard. Hairs stick up in excitement. The light follows the man who begins to pace around the podium, teasing enlightenment. 

"Before I reveal this, you must remember, the struggle will never finish. Even as I stand here, I too must continue my journey. The difference is that I ensure my journey is up. Friends, the secret is so simple, do not hate me for having heard it before. Even if you have, the reason you are here today is because you did not understand it the first time. Let me explain."

The next few minutes are mute. Not really of course, but the setting is so quiet, it's as if the little mites and insects have stopped scattering about to pay attention as well. The charisma is enchanting and caresses whoever is around. The sole thing heard is that of the secret. With each syllable of each word projected from his person, neurons snap and fire. Connections quickly restructuring for an epiphany. The speaker finishes, and the rejuvenation is complete. Music erupts just as the thunderous applauds cascade throughout. The speaker remains humble and stands still with a kind smile. The modesty is adored and the splendour is felt. Chants and hoorahs as emotions allow for release. A gasp of air never felt before pulled down channels rarely used before.

"Thank you. Always remember WHO YOU CAN BE!"

With that he walks out and away. The beast of the crowd yearns for his philosophy once more, he knows it. They chant and cheer, he hears it. But he cannot give in to the temptation to seize it for himself. For that would be against all he's taught.

He enters the car, waiting politely outside. "Up next, Toledo." The driver nods and the engine runs. He takes his throat medicine handily and rests for the long night trip. The gentle turbulence lulls him to sleep.

"There's something you must hear."

The vapid stares all point to the stage. The older man remembers the feeling of all the attention too. The rush of being the centre of it all. The thrill of knowing the audience listens and believes every word you say. At one point he lived for that feeling, chasing it as best as he could. The younger man too embodies that, he can tell. The older man clenches his fist and grits his teeth. His hair neatly trimmed for the first time in years. The smell of black hair dye to hide his greying streaks. The neatly pressed suit struggling against his wider waist. This is his first presentation in how long? But aside from the anxiety, he couldn't help but feel a budding anger towards the younger man. Every word and meaning was stolen from him. He is the original, staring down at his thief. But things are worse than that. The younger man not only took the older's material. He made it all better.
